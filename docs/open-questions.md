# Open Questions

> Intake queue for the dedicated project session. These are the questions that most affect scope,
> demo strategy, and delivery risk.

Last updated: 2026-03-12

## Confirmed by Organizer Orientation Recap

- Teams must have 2-5 members and at least one designated team lead
- Team formation, use case, and environment lock ran from March 6-March 11, 2026
- Solutioning sprint runs from March 12-March 25, 2026
- Final solutions and artifacts lock on March 25, 2026 at 5:00 PM; live demos and judging occur on March 27, 2026
- Orientation Q&A described a technical evaluation step on March 26, 2026
- Only original work created during the hackathon is eligible, and open-source tools or libraries must be disclosed
- AI tools are allowed if they are disclosed and team members understand the resulting code
- Submission must include enough material for review, such as a demo, README, or presentation
- Repositories may remain private if judges and the AI working group can access them
- At least one team member must be available during the judging window
- Respectful communication is required; plagiarism, cheating, harassment, and discrimination can result in disqualification

## Team and Ownership

1. Is this roster now final, and who is the designated team lead?
2. Who besides you will own data work, demo UI, architecture deck, and the 5-minute briefing?
3. Which people from the AI working committee can act as domain reviewers, sponsors, or fast
   feedback channels during the sprint?

## Delivery Format

4. Beyond the minimum demo, README, or presentation language, what exact artifact bundle does this
   team want ready by March 25?
5. Does the team want to provide a recorded demo anyway, even if it may not be strictly required?
6. What exact mechanism will be used to give judges and the AI working group access to the codebase?

## Technical Scope

7. Do you want the MVP centered on Medicare Part B provider behavior only, or Part B plus Part D
   from the beginning?
8. Is network analysis of provider relationships in scope for this sprint, or should it stay as an
   extension-path item?
9. Do you want a conservative, interpretable scoring stack first, or are you aiming for a more
   ambitious ensemble if time permits?

## Environment and Constraints

10. Which cloud environment, sandbox, or sponsor tooling is the team actually using for the sprint?
11. Will the solution need any special hardware, add-ons, or local dependencies that reviewers must know about?
12. Are there any committee or organizer expectations around responsible AI documentation beyond a
    fairness review, explainability, and AI-tool disclosure?

## Strategic Positioning

13. Which end user should the demo target most clearly: CMS program analyst, UPIC investigator, or
    executive decision-maker?
14. Do you already know which parts of the challenge story resonate best with organizers: improper
    payment savings, investigator efficiency, transparency, or pilot feasibility?
15. What non-public context do you already have from people you know that should shape the build
    plan early?

## Repo and Workflow

16. Now that the sprint window is active, should the next session immediately start implementation
    inside this project?
17. If implementation starts here, do you want to keep everything in this incubating repo or spin
    out a dedicated hackathon repo quickly?
