Hackathon Rules (part 1)
Eligibility & Team Formation
●
●
●
●

Team size limits (e.g., 2–5 members per team)
Participants must register by the deadline
Each person can only be on one team
Teams must have at least one designated team lead

Intellectual Property & Originality
●
●
●
●

All work must be original and built during the Hackathon period
No pre-built projects or significant prior work submitted as new
Teams retain ownership of their work
Any open-source tools/libraries must be disclosed

Code of Conduct
●
●
●
●

Respectful communication among all participants
No plagiarism or cheating
No harassment or discrimination of any kind
Violations result in disqualification

Hackathon Rules (part 2)
Submission Requirements
●
●
●
●

Projects must be submitted by the deadline (no exceptions)
Must include a demo, README, or presentation
Code must be pushed to a shared repository (e.g., GitHub)
Teams must present/demo their project to judges

Judging Criteria
●
●
●
●

Innovation & creativity
Technical complexity
Practicality / real-world impact
Quality of presentation/demo

Use of AI Tools
●
●

Disclose any AI tools used (e.g., GitHub Copilot, ChatGPT)
AI-generated code must be reviewed and understood by the team

General
●
●
●

Judges' decisions are final
Organizers reserve the right to disqualify teams for rule violations
Participants must be available during judging windows
