## What The Organizers Explicitly Confirmed

- Teams are 2-5 members and each team needs a designated lead
- All work must be original and built during the hackathon period
- Pre-built projects or significant prior work are not allowed as new submissions
- Teams retain ownership of their work
- Open-source tools and libraries must be disclosed
- AI tools may be used, but they must be disclosed and the team must understand the generated code
- Respectful communication is required; plagiarism, cheating, harassment, and discrimination can
  lead to disqualification
- Judges' decisions are final
- At least one team member must be available during the judging window and on demo day

## Submission And Judging Guidance

- Submission must include enough material for fair evaluation: demo, README, or presentation
- Code must be pushed to a shared repository
- The repository does not need to be public, but judges and the AI working group need access
- Organizers described judging as two parts:
  - technical evaluation after submission lock
  - live demo and presentation on March 27
- Live demo slots were described as roughly 5-7 minutes per team, with logistics still to be
  finalized

## Environment And Tooling Guidance

- Teams can use their preferred environment or provider; organizers asked teams to declare the
  chosen environment
- Local-machine or laptop-first solutions are acceptable if the code can still be shared and
  reviewed
- If a solution needs special hardware or add-ons, organizers want advance notice before review
- Office hours are intended to run nearly daily during the sprint
